## Free Academic Use

FoxML Core is **free for qualifying non-commercial academic research** at tax-exempt educational institutions.

### Qualifying Academic Use

You may use FoxML Core for free if:

- You are at a **degree-granting educational or non-profit research institution**
- The institution is **recognized as tax-exempt** under applicable law
- The institution is **not owned, controlled by, or operated for the benefit of any commercial entity**
- Research results are **not used to support commercial operations, trading, revenue-generating activities, or any business purpose**

## Common Questions

### Q: Can my PhD student use this for their dissertation research?

**A:** Yes, if the research is:
- Non-commercial
- Not funded by or integrated into commercial operations
- Not supporting revenue-generating activities
- At a qualifying tax-exempt institution

If the research is corporate-funded or supports commercial operations, a commercial license is required.

### Q: Can we use this in a class?

**A:** Yes, for academic coursework at qualifying tax-exempt educational institutions. If the class is part of a for-profit university or corporate training program, a commercial license is required.

### Q: What about sponsored research?

**A:** Research funded by, sponsored by, or integrated into commercial organizations requires a commercial license, even if performed at a non-profit institution.

### Q: Can students use this for startups?

**A:** No. Commercial use by students (e.g., for startups, client work, or revenue-generating activities) requires a commercial license.

### Q: What if the university uses it institutionally?

**A:** Any institutional use (internal tooling, dashboards, operational systems) requires a commercial license, even at non-profit institutions.
